Nest Egg Optimizer - System Documentation

The Nest Egg Optimizer is a web application designed to model, simulate, and optimize retirement portfolio withdrawals. It helps users determine the most tax-efficient sequence and ratio of withdrawals from various account types (Tax-Free, Tax-Deferred, and Taxable) to maximize the longevity of their retirement nest egg.

🗺️ Repository Layout

The repository is organized as a monorepo containing the following components:

  • demo-client/: Angular frontend client. Contains the UI layouts, interactive Chart.js widgets, and the browser-based client-side math and optimization engine.
  • demo-python/: Optional Python financial analysis/optimizer scripts used for backend verification.
  • DOCUMENTATION.md: Markdown documentation of the system.

⚙️ Architecture & Components

All optimization and calculations are executed client-side inside the browser for zero-latency, private, and serverless operation.

Key Frontend Modules:

  • UserInputComponent: Collects parameters (TFSA, RRSP, Margin, Return Rates, Inflation, Spend) and supports spinner controls to adjust rates in 0.5% increments.
  • LocalOptimizerService: The client-side optimization engine that calculates tax-minimized withdrawals and portfolio lifespans.
  • ChartComponent: Renders progressive burndown lines, caps withdraws to prevent showing withdrawals exceeding starting balances, and clamps year hover navigation.
  • BarChartComponent: Renders Monte Carlo simulation results, bell curves, typeable volatility parameters, and typeable success target years.

📈 Optimization Algorithm

To find the optimal withdrawal strategy, the system uses a **recursive multi-stage local search**:

  1. Permutation Testing: Generates combinations of withdrawal percentages across active accounts (e.g. Stage 1: TFSA 20%, RRSP 80%).
  2. Depletion Detection: Simulates the burndown timeline year-by-year until the first account runs out. This year is marked as a phase boundary.
  3. Recursion: Shrinks the active account set (excluding the depleted account), updates starting balances, and recursively invokes the optimizer for the next phase.
  4. Longevity Evaluation: Compares the total years survived across permutations, keeping the allocation path that yields the longest portfolio life.

💵 Tax Logic & Account Types

The system models Ontario and Federal tax rules to perform a **reverse tax calculation** (calculating the required pre-tax withdrawal needed to satisfy a post-tax spending target):

Account Type Description Tax Treatment on Withdrawal
TFSA Tax-Free Savings Account 100% Tax-Free. Withdrawals do not affect taxable income.
RRSP Registered Retirement Savings Plan 100% Taxable. Treated as regular earned income.
Non-Reg / Margin Taxable Investment Account Only 50% of the capital gains portion is included in taxable income. Principal withdrawals are tax-free.

🇨🇦 Eligible Canadian Dividend Tax Rules

For Non-Registered accounts, dividends are paid out annually and receive special tax treatment:

  • Gross-up (38%): Dividend payments are grossed up by 38% and added to taxable income.
  • Dividend Tax Credit (DTC): A Federal tax credit of 15.02% of the grossed-up dividend plus an Ontario Provincial tax credit directly reduce taxes owed.
  • Growth Adjustment: Because dividends are paid out rather than reinvested in the portfolio principal, the active growth rate inside the burndown loop for non-registered assets is adjusted to Return Rate - Dividend Yield.

🎲 Monte Carlo Simulations

To stress-test your strategy against market volatility, the simulator runs 1,000 independent runs:

  • Normal Distributions: Expectation returns are modeled using standard deviation standard deviations (Risk Profiles) ranging from 0% (Cash) to 25% (Aggressive).
  • Statistical Metrics: Displays the 10th (Worst), 50th (Median), and 90th (Best) percentile survival periods.
  • Dynamic Target success rates: Supports custom typeable target success years (e.g. 40+ years) to instantly recalculate confidence metrics.
